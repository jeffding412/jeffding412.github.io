About Me

Hello World! I'm Jeffrey Ding and I'm a Computer Science at UCLA! My three passions in life are basketball, software development, and personal finance, specifically in that order. Sadly, I don't think one of those passions are my true calling...
Anyways, I'm an aspiring software developer and my dream is to build financial technology applications in order to improve the financial lives of millions of people.
But why financial technology? A long time ago, I realized my passion wasn't having a lot of money, it was making money, specifically growing it. I didn't have any particular need to spend my money, I just loved to watch my money grow through compound interest. Still, investmenting was merely an intellectual pursuit for me. However, due to financial technologies such as Robinhood and Betterment/Wealthfront, I decided to study Computer Science at UCLA.
However, during my time at UCLA, I discovered the college CS education was missing a practical aspect. It was only after creating a website for some friends at the University of Michigan, I discovered a passion for web development. These discoveries drove me to attend the Coding Dojo in San Jose during the summer of 2018. There I learned how to make full-stack web/mobile applications using three different technology stacks (Python, JavaScript, and Swift).
When I'm not coding, I'm brainstorming ways to get a return on investment for my money. Recently, I've been investing in Real Estate Crowdfunding and Blockchain! In addition to Computer Science, I also hope to minor in Accounting! When I'm not coding or investing, I'm getting involved in college ministry or hooping on the courts of Wooden and Hitch.
