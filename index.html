About Me

Hello World! I'm Jeffrey Ding and I'm studying Computer Science at UCLA! My three passions in life are basketball, software development, and personal finance, specifically in that order. Sadly, I don't think one of those passions are my true calling...
Anyways, I'm an aspiring software developer and my dream is to build financial technology applications in order to improve the financial lives of millions of people. And it'd be an added bonus if my tech can make its way onto the phones of a few NBA players.
Now why financial technology you may ask? A long time ago, I realized that my passion making money. I didn't have any particular want from money, it was just so much fun to watch my money grow through compound interest and come up with new investment ideas. This early pratical experience in investing led me to believe that I didn't need to learn more about it in college. Therefore, I decided to study Computer Science when I entered UCLA.
However, during my time at UCLA, I discovered that the college CS education was missing a practical application aspect. Additionally, after creating a website for some friends at the University of Michigan, I discovered a passion for web development. These discoveries drove me to attend the Coding Dojo in San Jose during the summer before my sophomore year. There I learned how to make full-stack web/mobile applications using three different technology stacks (Python, JavaScript, and Swift).
When I'm not coding, I'm brainstorming ways to get a return on investment for my money. Recently, I've been investing in Real Estate Crowdfunding and Blockchain! In addition to Computer Science, I also hope to minor in Accounting! When I'm not coding or investing, I'm getting involved in college ministry or hooping on the courts of Wooden and Hitch.
